Insurance Platform Redesign

From Broken Agent Workflows to Modular Product Architecture

🎯 Senior Designer — Design Lead📅 Dec 2024 – Present🏢 Dotin — Pasargad Insurance

The Challenge

Policy issuance platform used by hundreds of agents had high abandonment. Agents called support instead of completing digital flows. Additionally, no formalized UX process existed — analysts directed frontend to make UI changes without design review.

Discovery — Field Research

Contextual Inquiry with 6 Agents

Visited agent offices and watched them work. Key findings:

  • Agents had Post-it cheat sheets on monitors — UI didn't clarify required fields
  • Paper form filled first, then typed into system — double work
  • 23 fields on one page, validation only after submit — 6 errors at once
  • Field labels used insurance jargon — agents called each other to ask meanings
🔍Contextual inquiry findings / agent office photos
Replace with your research photos

Design Solutions

1. Progressive Disclosure Wizard

Restructured 23-field page into 5-step wizard: Customer → Vehicle/Property → Coverage → Premium → Review. Inline validation, smart defaults, auto-fill from national ID.

2. Modular Design System

Built reusable modules (customer info, risk assessment, coverage selector, premium calculator). New insurance products configure from tested modules — no redesign needed.

3. UX Process Framework

Authored organization's first UX Process Guide: Design Brief template, 6-stage process, RACI matrix, Design QA gate. Presented to all teams and secured leadership sign-off.

Digital Credit Product (0→1)

Progressive Disclosure Turned Regulatory Complexity Into Trust

🎯 Product Designer (sole)📅 Feb – Nov 2022🏢 Ewano — Hamrah Aval (50M+ users)

The Challenge

Launch a digital micro-credit product (up to 5M toman) within Iran's largest telecom super-app. 0→1 product, heavily regulated — every screen needed compliance approval. Core tension: how to make a regulated financial product feel simple and trustworthy?

Discovery

Analyzed 4 lending products. Conducted 5 user interviews (Mom Test). Key insight: the terms review screen was the biggest anxiety point — users trapped between needing money and not understanding agreements.

"The terms page is always so long — I just scroll and accept. Then I get surprised later."

Design — Progressive Disclosure

Instead of one giant terms page, distributed information across contextual moments:

  • Before applying: 3-sentence summary
  • During: only relevant terms per step
  • Before confirmation: visual summary, link to full legal
  • After disbursement: repayment dashboard with balance, next payment, history

Design System from Scratch

From 14 Button Styles to One Coherent System — and the Governance That Kept It Alive

🎯 Design System Lead📅 Apr 2022 – Dec 2024🏢 Ewano Super-App — Hamrah Aval

The Problem

8 product modules designed by different people: 14 button variants (should be 4), 5 "primary blue" hex values, 9 input styles. Developers spent significant sprint time asking "which version is correct?"

🎨"Wall of Inconsistency" — 14 buttons side by side
Replace with your audit screenshots

My Approach — 4 Phases

Phase 1: Audit (2 weeks)

Screenshotted every screen across all 8 modules. Cataloged every unique element. Presented the "wall of inconsistency" to leadership — instant buy-in.

Phase 2: Define (3 weeks)

Design tokens: color, typography, spacing, elevation, radius. Naming conventions. Design principles.

Phase 3: Build (8 weeks)

Complete component library using atomic design: atoms (buttons, inputs, icons) → molecules (form fields, search) → organisms (headers, cards, modals, navigation). Every component with Auto-Layout, Variants, clear naming.

Phase 4: Document & Govern (ongoing)

Usage guidelines, dos/don'ts, accessibility notes, code references. Governance: request process, review workflow, contribution guidelines, versioning, changelog.
